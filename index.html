Dr. Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives

Here's a timeline of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1914 – Born in Cresco, Iowa
  • 1933 – Leaves his family's farm to attend the University of Minnesota, thanks to a Depression-era program known as the "National Youth Administration"
  • 1935 – Stops school to save money. Works in the Civilian Conservation Corps, helping starving Americans. "I saw how food changed them", he said. "All of this left scars on me."
  • 1937 – Finishes university and joins the US Forestry Service
  • 1938 – Marries Margret Gibson. Laid off due to budget cuts. Inspired by Elvin Charles Stakman, returns to school to study plant breeding
  • 1941 – Tries to enlist after Pearl Harbor but is rejected. His lab is asked to work on glue, malaria control (DDT), and disinfectants
  • 1942 – Earns a Ph.D. in Genetics and Plant Pathology
  • 1944 – Turns down a salary increase from Dupont, moves to Mexico to lead a wheat pathology program
  • 1945 – Develops method to grow wheat twice a year
  • 1953 – Creates a dwarf, fertilizer-responsive strain of wheat — becomes 95% of Mexico’s wheat production
  • 1962 – Brings his wheat strains to India during population crisis
  • 1970 – Wins the Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1983 – Helps African countries boost maize and sorghum production
  • 1984 – Becomes professor at Texas A&M University
  • 2005 – Advocates genetically modified crops as the future of food security
  • 2009 – Dies at the age of 95

"Borlaug's life and achievement are testimony to the far-reaching contribution that one man's towering intellect, persistence and scientific vision can make to human peace and progress."

– Indian Prime Minister Manmohan Singh
